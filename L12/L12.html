Nuclear Power Plants (NPP)

A nuclear power plant (NPP) is a facility that produces electricity by using heat from nuclear fission. In the reactor, atoms of uranium are split, releasing a large amount of energy. This heat is used to turn water into steam, which spins a turbine connected to a generator. NPPs can produce large amounts of electricity with low greenhouse gas emissions, making them an important source of power in many countries.

Nuclear power plant also use control rods to regulate the nuclear reaction. These rods absorb neutrons and can be raised or lowered to keep the reaction stable. To prevent radioactive materials from escaping, reactors are built inside thick concrete and steel containment structures. Cooling systems are critical, because without proper cooling, the reactor core can overheat. Although nuclear energy is powerful and efficient, it requires strict safety systems and trained professionals to operate it safely.
